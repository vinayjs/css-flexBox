Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist, widely held to be one of the greatest and most influential scientists of all time.

Born

14 March 1879 Ulm, Kingdom of Württemberg, German Empire

Known For

General relativity Special relativity Photoelectric effect E=mc2 (mass–energy equivalence) E=hf (Planck–Einstein relation)

Awards

Barnard Medal (1920) Nobel Prize in Physics (1921) Matteucci Medal (1921) ForMemRS (1921)[1] Copley Medal (1925)[1] Gold Medal of the Royal Astronomical Society (1926)[2] Max Planck Medal (1929) Member of the National Academy of Sciences (1942)[3] Time Person of the Century (1999)
